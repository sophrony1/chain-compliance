What is "chain compliance"?

Chain compliance (a.k.a. feather fork): U.S.–aligned miners refuse sanctioned transactions and rewrite the chain to erase blocks that include them. With enough committed hashrate, mining a non-compliant block becomes a losing bet.

How to read the comparative statics
  • Rewrites cut both ways. Higher costs push non-compliant effort down; higher stakes per surviving block push it up. Slide θ up — activity rises before the profitable region collapses.
  • Fines are fixed costs. They shrink profits without shifting marginal strategy.
  • Energy decides. Sanctions work when compliant miners have cheaper energy. Migration to flared-gas or hydro flips the result.

Caveat: sanctioned users can offer higher fees to stay included, raising non-compliant fee revenue and potentially funding hashrate expansion (paper p. 14). The "works" verdict is short-run, fee-static.
